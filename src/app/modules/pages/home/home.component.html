Fun on your terms

We call it a Playhouse on purpose: it’s built for fun, competition, and rewards using Claimy as the in-app currency. That keeps the experience distinct from old-school gambling — everything runs on our native token economy, with balances you can see and move on Solana.

Games in the Playhouse

We’re building four games around the same credit model: each has a page in the app, and real-money play will call into Supabase Edge Functions and Postgres so bets, sessions, and payouts stay auditable. Here’s where each one stands and how the pieces fit together.

Flowerpoker

Five seeds, two rows, poker-style hands — black or white flowers force a reroll.

In the app

Angular UI for bet input, planting seeds left-to-right, and displaying the round the server sent — no local dice rolls; refresh resumes via saved planted count.

On the server

Edge flowerpoker-game: HMAC-SHA256 rolls from a committed server seed + your client seed, black/white rerolls, sessions in claimy_game_sessions, settlement and payouts via claimy_apply_credit_delta.

Blackjack

Classic 21-style play against the house — hit, stand, and settle hands.

In the app

Placeholder page today; the plan is a table UI with shoe/deck visuals, bet controls, and clear hand outcome messaging wired to the same wallet session.

On the server

Dedicated Edge + session flow (deal, hit, stand, settle) will mirror the Flowerpoker pattern: locked stakes, game rows in Postgres, atomic credit updates.

Dice

Roll-based outcomes with configurable targets and payout multipliers.

In the app

Placeholder page; frontend will expose roll animation, bet amount, target range, and history for the current session.

On the server

Server-side RNG or verifiable roll commitment, bet settlement, and balance updates through the same credits RPCs — implementation TBD alongside game rules.

Hi-low

Predict whether the next value is higher or lower — streak-friendly, fast rounds.

In the app

Placeholder page; UI will focus on the current card/value, higher/lower actions, and round-by-round feedback.

On the server

Backend will generate the sequence, enforce bet rules, and record outcomes to credits — same ledger discipline as the rest of the Playhouse.

Design goal

We’re aiming to work differently from a typical casino: our games will use 50/50 win chances — real coin-flip fairness — and the house only takes a small cut of the potential payout, not a hidden edge baked into the rules. Games that ship with a heavy built-in “game edge” can feel unfair; we’d rather keep odds transparent and modest.

Example payout

Example: you bet 10 Claimy on a round with a 50/50 shot to win. If you win, you might receive 19 Claimy back — the fair double would be 20; we keep a small slice (here, 1 Claimy) so the model is sustainable without crushing the player. The exact numbers can vary by game, but the idea is the same: fair odds plus a light fee on the upside, not a rigged table.

Future: PDA vault & $CLAIMY liquidity

Today we’re not yet on the full PDA vault path — that’s a Solana-native, program-controlled vault we plan for later. When $CLAIMY goes live, we intend to purchase 5% of total supply on the developer wallet and route that allocation into a dedicated vault meant to help fund payouts to players as volume grows.

Is 5% enough to cover every bet forever? No — not without limits. That’s why at the start we’ll need scaling and a max bet: so that if probability does what it does over the long run, we always keep enough margin to pay out our supporters. Bet caps are a safety rail for the treasury, not a trick — they help us stay solvent while the Playhouse and liquidity mature.
