Leaderboards & ranking

Every account starts at the lowest tier. Your lifetime SPL bet volume (total staked across Playhouse games) moves you up the ladder. Higher ranks take more volume to reach — and pay a slightly better multiplier on wins. Losses always cost your full stake.

Rank tiers & payouts

On a winning bet, return = stake × multiplier. Net profit on a win (per 1 unit staked) is multiplier − 1; on a loss you lose −1.00 per unit staked. The table uses your minimum lifetime wager to unlock that tier — we keep the full ladder in one place for transparency.

Why the gaps get bigger

Early jumps (Bronze → Iron) need modest volume so new players can see progress. Later tiers (Emerald → Diamond) require disproportionately more wagering — top ranks should feel rare, and each step should mean more than the last. Exact numbers may be tuned before launch; we will keep this page in sync.
