Flowerpoker

Rules, odds & provably fair

This page mirrors the server Edge function: same weights, same HMAC recipe, same digest. Use Verify round after a hand settles to compare values in your browser.

How a round plays

  1. You choose a bet in Claimy credits and receive a round with five hidden flowers for you and five for the house.
  2. You pick a client seed (or the app generates one). The server commits to SHA-256(server seed) before any flowers are shown.
  3. Click each Mithril seed left to right; each click reveals the matching slot on both rows.
  4. If any of the ten flowers is black or white, the round is void for scoring and the game requests a reroll (new sub-round, same seeds — see server).
  5. Otherwise the best five-flower poker hand wins: bust → five of a kind as on OSRS flower games.
  6. Win pays at least 1.9× your stake at the starter rank (e.g. 10 bet → 19 returned on a win). Higher rank improves the multiplier on wins; losses always cost the full stake.

Hand strength (high card wins)

Ties on rank go to the next rule in the list; identical hands are a tie.

  • 5 of a kind — five matching colours
  • 4 of a kind
  • Full house — three + pair
  • 3 of a kind
  • 2 pair
  • 1 pair
  • Bust — no pair

Why it is provably fair

  • The server picks a random 32-byte server seed and shows you only SHA-256(seed) until settlement.
  • Flowers are not “picked” by a black box: each of ten slots uses HMAC-SHA256(key = server seed bytes, message = fixed string), then maps the first four bytes to a float in [0,1) and walks the weight table (same as the table above).
  • A roll digest is published: SHA-256(playerIds|houseIds|subRound|clientSeed) so the exact board is committed together with your client seed and sub-round index.
  • After settlement, the base64 server seed is revealed. Anyone can hash it and re-run the HMAC steps offline.
